Demografy AI Agent

I answer questions about Australian suburb demographics and community signals. I do not use live house-price or property sales data; for affordability-style questions I can use proxies like rental access, home ownership, prosperity, social housing, and stability.

What you can ask · What each metric measures
  • Not included: live house prices, listings, crime, school rankings, transport access, amenities, forecasts, or income data.
  • Try: affluent suburbs in Victoria, educated suburbs in NSW, stable suburbs with high home ownership, or affordable rental suburbs in Queensland.
  • For a named area, ask: diversity index in Forde, home ownership in Berwick, or population in Parramatta.
  • Examples: top diverse suburbs in NSW, average prosperity in Victoria, diversity index in Forde, “what KPIs do you measure?”
  • After a list or ranking answer, type pie chart or use Show as a chart? to visualise.
Metric What it measures
Prosperity score Socioeconomic advantage and disadvantage for the area. Shown as an index from 0–100 (higher = more advantaged).
Diversity index Cultural and linguistic diversity. Scale 0–1; higher values mean more culturally diverse communities.
Migration footprint Share of residents with overseas-born parents. Expressed as 0–100%.
Learning level Educational attainment proxy (e.g. Year 12 or equivalent completion). 0–100%.
Social housing Share of households in public or community housing. 0–100%.
Home ownership (resident equity) Share of households that own outright or with a mortgage. 0–100%.
Rental access Rental affordability proxy—share renting below a modest weekly rent threshold. 0–100%.
Resident anchor (stability) Share of residents who lived at the same address five years earlier. 0–100%.
Household mobility Households in transitional living situations. Index 0–1.
Young families Share of residents aged 0–14 (young-family presence). 0–100%.
Population Estimated resident population for the area (headcount).
